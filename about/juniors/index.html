Juniors

Where the next generation of underwater hockey players begin. Our Sunday sessions focus on water confidence, safe technique, teamwork, and real game skills. Building pathways from first session all the way to BOA Junior Regionals, Nationals, and beyond to GB U19 and U24 selection.

Safeguarding & welfare

We take the safety and wellbeing of every junior player seriously. Our approach is aligned with BOA guidance and good practice.

  • DBS-checked coaches

    All junior coaches hold valid DBS checks. Sessions are supervised and structured for safety.

  • Parent/guardian registration

    Under-18 players require parent or guardian-led registration, including declaration of any relevant medical conditions (BOA-aligned).

  • Clear welfare contact

    Our Club Welfare Officer is Alan Underwood Speak to any coach poolside or contact Alan directly with any concerns.

  • Risk management

    We follow BOA-aligned controlled risk and club risk assessment approaches to keep sessions safe and well-managed.

  • Player-focused environment

    Safe learning, confidence building, positive coaching, and respectful conduct for everyone in the pool.

  • Concussion awareness

    We follow BOA concussion guidance. If in doubt, sit it out - safety always comes first.

  • Data privacy

    Registration details are handled carefully and used only for club administration and welfare purposes.

References: Our safeguarding approach is aligned with BOA (British Octopush Association) guidance, including the Child Protection & Safeguarding Policy, Controlled Risks Assessment, Club Risk Assessment, and Concussion Guidance. View BOA Safeguarding & Welfare resources →

The Juniors journey

From first session to competition.

First session

Water confidence, safety basics, and getting comfortable with kit.

Skills building

Finning technique, duck-dives, stick and puck control.

Team play

Positioning, communication, and understanding substitutions.

Match readiness

Friendly games, regular club sessions, building game sense.

Competition

BOA Junior Regionals and National tournament experience.

Next level

Regional squads, GB U19 and U24 trials the pathway continues.

Training that travels

Our juniors regularly compete at BOA Junior Regionals and Nationals and some have gone on to represent regional squads and earn selection for GB U19 and U24 teams.

Those medals and experiences don't just happen. They come from consistent sessions, patient coaching, and a community that encourages every player to push a little further each week.

Whether it's a first tournament or a step toward international trials, we're proud to be part of the journey.
